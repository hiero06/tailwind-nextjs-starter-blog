## Discover Regency Romance Beyond Bridgerton: 12 Captivating Books and Characters to Fall For

As "Bridgerton" season four prepares to enthrall audiences with Benedict Bridgerton's love story, fans are eagerly anticipating a return to the opulent Regency era. For those captivated by its blend of romance and intrigue, a wealth of historical romance novels awaits exploration. These tales feature everything from clandestine affairs to bold, spirited characters—including 'Ned Lightbourne,' a figure who might particularly appeal to readers searching for a compelling 'Ned Luke' type character.

### Event/Topic Overview

The beloved Netflix series "Bridgerton" is set to release its fourth season, focusing on Benedict Bridgerton, the family's second-eldest son. This season will adapt Julia Quinn's third book, "An Offer from a Gentleman," which tells the enchanting, Cinderella-esque tale of Benedict and Sophie Beckett, a maid with a mysterious past.

The highly anticipated season four will be released in two parts, with the first episodes premiering on January 29, 2026. Viewers will then wait until February 26 for the second half of the season, promising a full month of Regency-era drama and romance.

### Key Highlights & Standouts

#### Benedict and Sophie's Story: An Offer from a Gentleman

Julia Quinn's novel, "An Offer from a Gentleman," forms the basis of Benedict's love story. It introduces Sophie, an illegitimate child raised as a ward who is later forced into servitude by her cruel stepmother. A single night of freedom at a Bridgerton masquerade ball leads to an unforgettable encounter with Benedict, who, unaware of her true identity, is instantly smitten. Years later, their paths cross again when Sophie is working as a maid. Though Benedict doesn't recognize her, he is irresistibly drawn to her, setting the stage for a tale of forbidden love and yearning.

#### "Bringing Down the Duke" by Evie Dunmore

The first book in "A League of Extraordinary Women" series, this novel follows Annabelle Archer, one of the first women to attend Oxford in 1879. Annabelle's mission to recruit influential men for the suffragette movement leads her to Sebastian Devereux, the Duke of Montgomery, and an unexpected and powerful attraction blossoms between them, challenging their respective goals.

#### "The Davenports" by Krystal Marquis

For fans of "Bridgerton's" ensemble cast, "The Davenports" offers a rich tapestry of interconnected love stories. Inspired by the 'Golden Age' of Black society, it follows Olivia, Helen, their former maid Amy-Rose, and Olivia's best friend Ruby as they navigate romance, societal expectations, and burgeoning attractions in 1910 Chicago.

#### "Outlander" by Diana Gabaldon

Like "Bridgerton," "Outlander" also transitioned from a successful book series to a popular TV show. This nine-book saga infuses historical romance with a touch of magic, as former combat nurse Claire Randall is mysteriously transported from 1945 to 1743 Scotland, where she falls for the courageous Highlander warrior Jamie Fraser.

#### "The Magpie Lord" by KJ Charles

Combining aristocratic intrigue with dark magic, "The Magpie Lord" introduces Lucien Vaudrey, who returns to London as Lord Crane following the sudden deaths of his father and brother. He enlists the help of magician Stephen Day to combat nefarious magic, and as their investigation deepens, Lucien finds himself irresistibly drawn to Stephen, risking both their lives amidst their burgeoning connection.

#### "Suddenly You" by Lisa Kleypas

Author Amanda Briars decides to explore intimacy as a unique 30th birthday gift to herself. An encounter with publisher Jack Delvin leads to an unanticipated night of passion, followed by a professional collaboration that forces them to confront their undeniable attraction, despite their differing backgrounds.

#### "The Wisteria Society of Lady Scoundrels" by India Holton (featuring Ned Lightbourne)

This novel offers a darkly adventurous twist to historical romance. Cecilia Bassingwaite, a demure Victorian woman by day, is secretly a cunning thief and member of the Wisteria Society. Her world collides with the formidable assassin-for-hire Ned Lightbourne, who has been tasked with eliminating her. However, their shared mission to save the Wisteria Society from the villainous Captain Morvath ignites an unexpected connection, solidifying Ned Lightbourne as a compelling figure in this thrilling tale.

#### "Rebel" by Beverly Jenkins

Set in Reconstruction-era New Orleans, "Rebel" introduces Valinda Lacy, a determined woman from New York City seeking to build a new life. When her school is targeted, architect Captain Drake LeVeq steps in to help, instantly smitten by her independence and spirit. Despite a fiancé waiting for her in New York, Valinda's growing feelings for Drake begin to challenge her carefully laid plans.

#### "The Duchess Deal" by Tessa Dare

Perfect for fans of marriage-of-convenience tropes, "The Duchess Deal" centers on the scarred Duke of Ashbury, who desperately needs a wife to produce an heir. When his fiancée abandons him, seamstress Emma Gladstone steps in as a surprising replacement, gradually breaking down the Duke's formidable defenses as they navigate their unconventional arrangement.

#### "Ana María and the Fox" by Liana De la Rosa

During a Victorian London social season, Mexican heiress Ana María Luna Valdés encounters Gideon Fox, a charismatic politician dedicated to ending the Atlantic slave trade. Though Gideon believes he has no time for romance, he finds himself irresistibly drawn to Ana and compelled to protect her, igniting a passionate love story against a backdrop of political intrigue.

#### "A Night Like This" by Julia Quinn

Another enchanting novel from Julia Quinn's "Smythe-Smith Quartet," this book explores class tension through the eyes of governess Anne Wynter, a woman meticulously guarding her true identity. Her resolve is tested when Daniel Smythe-Smith, the Earl of Winstead, returns to town and sets his sights on her, challenging her to confront unexpected feelings.

#### "Slightly Married" by Mary Balogh

The first in the "Bedwyn Saga," this book follows Colonel Aidan Bedwyn, a seemingly tough soldier with a surprising soft heart. After the death of one of his soldiers, he visits the man's sister, Eve, intending to offer assistance. Instead, he finds an enchanting and fearless woman who agrees to a loveless marriage to save her home, only for a single, unexpected kiss to unravel their carefully constructed plans.

The recommendations of these twelve additional books further underscore the enduring appeal and high regard for the historical romance genre among readers and viewers alike.
