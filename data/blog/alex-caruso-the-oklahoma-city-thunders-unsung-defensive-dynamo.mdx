Over a recent four-game stretch, which included three matchups against playoff-bound teams, Alex Caruso tallied an impressive 12 steals, averaging a remarkable 3.0 steals per game. This statistic powerfully illustrates his ability to disrupt opposing offenses and generate critical turnovers, making him a central figure in the Thunder's defensive strategy.

Despite a two-point loss for Oklahoma City, Caruso logged 24 minutes—his second-highest minute count of the season—and finished with a stellar +22 plus/minus rating. This significant figure unequivocally demonstrates how profoundly the Thunder benefit from his time on the court.

This external validation further solidifies his reputation as a disruptive force across the league.
