### Key Highlights & Standouts

#### Week 16 Must-Starts: Elite Performers

For those looking to secure a victory, several players stand out as elite options. At quarterback, Jalen Hurts (Eagles), Josh Allen (Bills), and Lamar Jackson (Ravens) are top-tier choices. Running back selections include Bijan Robinson (Falcons), James Cook (Bills), Jahmyr Gibbs (Lions), and De'Von Achane (Dolphins), all projected for high-end production. Wide receivers like Amon-Ra St. Brown (Lions), Ja'Marr Chase (Bengals), and Mike Evans (Buccaneers) are considered must-starts, while Trey McBride (Cardinals) and George Kittle (49ers) lead the tight end ranks. The Houston Texans and Buffalo Bills defenses are also strong plays this week.

#### Start 'Em Decisions: Upside Plays

Several players offer significant upside for Week 16. Quarterback C.J. Stroud (Texans) is a top DFS value pick against the Raiders, coming off a strong 260-yard, three-touchdown performance. Jameson Williams (Lions) is highlighted as a value receiver against a Steelers secondary missing key players. In the backfield, Quinshon Judkins (Browns) faces a porous Bills run defense, while Woody Marks (Texans) is expected to play fully and could see high volume against the Raiders. DK Metcalf (Seahawks) has a favorable indoor matchup against a Lions defense allowing the second-most fantasy points to receivers, making him an excellent choice. Dallas Goedert (Eagles) has been on a tear, and Tyler Warren (Colts) is a solid PPR option, especially with Philip Rivers at QB.

#### Sit 'Em Considerations: Risky Bets

Conversely, some prominent names carry considerable risk for Week 16. Joe Burrow (Bengals), Dak Prescott (Cowboys), and Trevor Lawrence (Jaguars) are recommended sits at quarterback due to challenging matchups or recent performance trends. Running backs like RJ Harvey (Broncos) and Chase Brown (Bengals) face tough run defenses, while Ashton Jeanty (Raiders) is limited by a struggling offense. Among wide receivers, Wan'Dale Robinson (Giants) and Jaylen Waddle (Dolphins) face difficult secondaries or quarterback uncertainty. Tight ends like Mark Andrews and Isaiah Likely (Ravens), Dalton Kincaid (Bills), and Harold Fannin Jr. (Browns) also pose risks due to matchups or rookie QB situations.
