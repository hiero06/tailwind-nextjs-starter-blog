## Air India Plane Crash in Ahmedabad: Hundreds Feared Dead in Latest Aviation Tragedy

A devastating air india plane crashes has occurred near Ahmedabad, India, involving an Air India flight bound for London. The crash, minutes after takeoff, has resulted in a significant loss of life and prompted immediate responses from both Indian and international authorities.

### Quick Context

On June 12, 2025, Air India flight AI171, en route from Ahmedabad to London Gatwick, crashed shortly after takeoff. The Boeing 787-8 Dreamliner, carrying 242 passengers and crew, went down in a residential area, hitting a medical college hostel during lunch hour. The incident has brought immediate focus on air india plane crashes and aviation safety protocols.

#### Surprising Fact: The plane crashed on top of the dining area of a state-run B.J. Medical College hostel, potentially impacting medical students.

Initial reports indicate a "Mayday" call was issued by the aircraft to air traffic control, signaling an emergency. However, communication was lost shortly thereafter, raising questions about the sequence of events leading to the crash. Rescue efforts are underway, with authorities working to recover bodies and provide assistance to the affected families.

#### Key Statistic: 242 passengers and crew members were aboard the flight AI171, including 217 adults, 11 children, and two infants.

### What You Need to Know

The Air India flight AI171 crash is a major aviation incident with far-reaching consequences. Here's a breakdown of the key information available so far:

#### Core Development: The Crash

The Air India Boeing 787-8 Dreamliner crashed minutes after taking off from Ahmedabad Airport. The plane crashed into a residential area, specifically a medical college hostel, causing significant damage and casualties on the ground.

#### Core Development: Passengers and Crew

Onboard were 242 individuals, comprising passengers and crew. The manifest included 169 Indian nationals, 53 Britons, seven Portuguese citizens, and one Canadian. The flight was under the command of Captain Sumeet Sabharwal, with First Officer Clive Kundar as his co-pilot.

#### Core Development: Immediate Response

Following the crash, fire tenders were dispatched to the site to extinguish the blaze. Rescue operations were initiated, and injured individuals were transported to hospitals. Authorities have asked relatives to provide DNA samples to aid in the identification of the deceased.

#### Core Development: Official Statements and Actions

Air India confirmed the accident and established a helpline for passenger assistance (1800 5691 444). The Ministry of Civil Aviation also set up helpline numbers (011-24610843 and 9650391859). Ahmedabad City Police issued an emergency number (07925620359). The British government provided a helpline for UK citizens (020 7008 5000).

#### Core Development: Investigation and Recovery

The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) has initiated an investigation into the crash. Black box data will be analyzed to determine the cause. Three National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) teams were mobilized to the crash site to assist in rescue and recovery efforts.
